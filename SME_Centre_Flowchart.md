## 3. Cash Flow Timeline – Demo Script

**What is on screen**
- 4 stat cards: Total Inflow, Total Outflow, Net Cashflow, Negative Months.
- 12-month composed chart:
  - Green bars = Inflow
  - Red bars = Outflow
  - Green/red net bars = month-wise surplus/shortage
  - Blue line = net trend

**What to say**
> "This is the Cash Flow Timeline for Dr. Neha Gupta's dermatology clinic. Over the year the business sees ₹246.8L inflow and ₹225.8L outflow, leaving a net surplus of ₹21.0L. But the headline number hides seasonality. Look at the red months — May and August — where outflow exceeded inflow. The green months show surplus that we can deploy later."

**Point out**
- Green net bars = surplus months.
- Red net bars = shortage months.
- The blue line connects the net trend across the year.
- The amber alert below the chart: "Liquidity alert: 2 month(s) show cash outflow exceeding inflow."

---

## 4. Working Capital Health – Demo Script

**What is on screen**
- Working capital score (e.g., 78/100, Grade B+).
- Colour-coded ratio cards:
  - **Current Ratio** – green if ≥ 1.5, amber if 1.2–1.5, red if < 1.2
  - **Quick Ratio** – green if ≥ 1.0, amber if 0.8–1.0, red if < 0.8
- Cards for Inventory Days, Receivable Days, Payable Days.

**What to say**
> "Working Capital Health gives a one-glance diagnostic. The score is 78 out of 100, grade B+. The current ratio is green, so short-term obligations are comfortably covered. The quick ratio is amber — still okay, but it tells us a chunk of liquidity is tied up in inventory or receivables. These colour codes let a non-finance business owner instantly know where to act."

---

## 5. Surplus Fund Advisor – Demo Script

**What is on screen**
- Current surplus amount (e.g., ₹5,50,000).
- Three ranked recommendations with projected value and risk:
  1. Sweep to 91-day Corporate FD — ₹3,00,000 at 7.4% → ₹3,05,550 (low risk)
  2. Liquid Mutual Fund — ₹1,50,000 at 6.8% → ₹1,52,550 (low risk)
  3. Prepay high-cost vendor credit — ₹1,00,000 at 14% saved → ₹1,14,000 (zero risk)

**What to say**
> "Finally, the Surplus Fund Advisor turns idle cash into returns. Instead of letting ₹5.5L sit in a current account, the engine recommends a ladder: sweep the biggest chunk into a 91-day corporate FD, keep liquidity in a liquid fund, and use the remainder to prepay 14% vendor credit. The projected values are shown right here."
